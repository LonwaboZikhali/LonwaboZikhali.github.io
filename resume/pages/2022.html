In the year 2022, I graduated with a Bachelor of Health Science in Biomedical Science, majoring in Human Biology and Medical Cell Biology. This degree equipped me with in-depth knowledge of human biology, disease mechanisms, and research methodologies, alongside hands-on experience with advanced laboratory techniques.

My key areas of expertise:

  • - body systems, their functions, and interactions
  • - molecular mechanisms driving cellular function and disease
  • - immune system disorders and the biological basis of diseases
  • - drug mechanisms and interactions within the body
  • - disease patterns in populations and strategies for prevention and control

In Medical Cell Biology, I explored the intricate functions of cells and how they contribute to health and disease. This included an in-depth study of neurobiology, the cardiovascular system, cancer, and embryology. These areas helped me understand the mechanisms driving key physiological and pathological processes, giving me insight into how cellular changes impact the body.


In Human Biology, I studied the fascinating fields of paleontology, forensic science, anthropology, and evolution, with a focus on the analysis of bones and their significance in understanding human history. These subjects provided me with an appreciation for the diversity of life, human origins, and the application of biological principles in solving modern forensic challenges.
